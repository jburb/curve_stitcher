Welcome to Triangula!

In Triangula, you can explore how a special tool called "recursion" can be used to create shapes like the Sierpinski triangle, where one change is repeated to make a growing set of similar shapes out of a simple starting shape.

The Sierpinski triangle is named for Waclaw Sierpinski, who discovered it in the early 20th century. Sierpinski was fascinated by recursive or "fractal" shapes, which are "self-similar", meaning when you zoom in, smaller copies of the whole shape are revealed, again and again. Sierpinski's work with fractals led him to also discover the Sierpinski carpet (seen above), and the Sierpinski space-filling curve (seen below).

Fractals like these three are made by applying a recursive rule to a shape, a recursive rule being any rule that you re-apply to its own output until some stopping point is met.

To see recursion at its clearest in Triangula, use parallel fractal mode and choose to repeat by cutting, with 1st band coloring.
